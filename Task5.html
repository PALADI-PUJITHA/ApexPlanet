Taj Mahal,Agra(Uttar Pradesh)

The Taj Mahal,located in Agra,Uttar Pradesh, is one of the most famous monuments in the world and a UNESCO world Heritage site. Built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Muntaz Mahal, it was constructed in the period of 1632-1653. It stands as a symbol of eternal love and architectural brilliance.

Jaipur and Amer Fort

Jaipur, known as the "Pilnk City", is the capital of Rajasthan and a UNESCO World Heritage City. Renowned for its royal heritage, grand architecture, and vibrant culture, Jaipur is a must-visit destination. A key highlight is Amer Fort(Amer Fort)

Kerala Backwaters

The Kerala Backwaters are a unique network of lagoons,lakes,rivers and canals along the Malabar Coast in Kerala, India. This tranquil ecosystem is a top tourist attraction,offering an immersive experience of Kerala's natural beauty and culture.

Varanasi(Uttar Pradesh)

Varanasi,also known as Kashi or Banaras,is one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities and a major cultural and spiritual hub in Uttar Pradesh,India. Situated on the banks of the sacred River Ganges,it is regarded as the spiritual capital of India and attracts pilgrims and tourists from all over the world

Leh-Ladakh(Jammu&Kashmir)

Leh-Ladakh, located in the northern most region of India, is a land of rugged beauty, ancient monasteries and breathtaking landscapes. Situated in the state of Jammu and Kashmir,it is often referred to as the "Land of High Passes" due to its vast mountain ranges, including the Himalayas and Zanskar ranges.
